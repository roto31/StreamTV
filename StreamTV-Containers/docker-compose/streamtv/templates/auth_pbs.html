Browser Cookies Authentication (Recommended)

Export cookies from your browser to access PBS live streams and Passport content.

info
Why is this needed?

PBS live streams and Passport content require authentication. Using browser cookies is the most reliable method to access protected streams.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Install a cookie export extension:
    • Chrome/Edge: Get cookies.txt LOCALLY
    • Firefox: cookies.txt
    • Safari: Export Cookies
  2. Log into PBS:
    • Go to account.pbs.org/accounts/login/
    • Sign in with your PBS Passport credentials
    • Or log into your local PBS station
  3. Visit a live stream page:
    • Navigate to a PBS live stream page
    • Example: Visit your local PBS station's live stream page
    • Verify you can access the stream
  4. Export cookies:
    • Click the cookie extension icon in your browser
    • Select "Export" or "Download as cookies.txt"
    • Make sure to export cookies for the PBS domain (pbs.org, etc.)
    • Save the file to your computer
  5. Upload below:
Select your exported cookies.txt file from PBS

Supported PBS Stations

PBS authentication works with:

  • PBS Passport: pbs.org/passport/
  • Other PBS Stations: Most local PBS stations with live streaming

What Cookies Are Used For

The cookies file contains:

  • pbs_uid - User ID
  • pbs_sid - Session ID
  • pbs_authtoken - Authentication token
  • Other session cookies - Session data

Security: Your cookies are stored locally and never shared. They work just like your browser session.

Expiration: Cookies typically last 30-90 days. When they expire, just export fresh cookies from your browser.
